IIHS: Teenagers

Insurance Institute for Highway Safety (IIHS)

The IIHS is a U.S. nonprofit organization dedicated to reducing the number of vehicle crashes and injuries. It conducts extensive research and testing to provide evidence-based safety recommendations and ratings for vehicles.

Summary

The IIHS provides comprehensive data on teenage driving behaviors and crash statistics, emphasizing the high risk of accidents among young drivers. The organization highlights the effectiveness of graduated driver licensing (GDL) programs in reducing teen crash rates. It also stresses the importance of parental supervision and setting clear rules for teen drivers. The IIHS research includes vehicle safety ratings, helping parents choose safer cars for their teenagers. The source underscores the need for ongoing education and awareness campaigns to promote safe driving habits. Overall, the IIHS aims to create safer driving environments for teenagers through rigorous research and advocacy.
