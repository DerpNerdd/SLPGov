NICHD: Teen Driving Risk

National Institute of Child Health and Human Development (NICHD)

The NICHD is part of the National Institutes of Health (NIH), focusing on conducting and supporting research on the health and development of children, including studies on teen driving risks and safety.

Summary

The NICHD investigates the various risk factors associated with teen driving, emphasizing the developmental and psychological aspects that contribute to unsafe driving behaviors. Their research highlights the influence of peer pressure and the lack of experience in increasing crash risks among teens. The NICHD advocates for educational programs tailored to young drivers to address these vulnerabilities. The institute also explores the role of technology and policy in enhancing teen driving safety. By examining the multifaceted nature of teen driving risks, the NICHD provides insights into effective interventions. Their work supports the development of comprehensive strategies to safeguard young drivers on the road.
